# High Performance for Entrepreneurs — Brendon Burchard

## Core Principle
Success is not about talent or luck. It's about deliberate habits practiced consistently. The six High Performance Habits — Clarity, Energy, Necessity, Productivity, Influence, and Courage — are the operating system for entrepreneurial excellence.

## Clarity for Entrepreneurs

### The Clarity Stack
Before strategy, before tactics, before action — clarity.

**Clarity on Self:**
- "What are my strengths that I should double down on?"
- "What are my weaknesses that I need to delegate?"
- "What kind of entrepreneur do I want to be known as?"

**Clarity on Offer:**
- "What is the ONE transformation I deliver?"
- "Who is the exact person I serve best?"
- "What makes my approach different from everything else?"

**Clarity on Vision:**
- "Where is this business in 3 years?"
- "What does my ideal day look like when I've built this?"
- "What would I build if I knew I couldn't fail?"

### The 3-Word Identity
Choose 3 words that define who you need to BE to achieve your goals.
- Example: "Bold. Disciplined. Generous."
- Write them down. Review them every morning.
- Before every decision, ask: "Would a bold, disciplined, generous person do this?"

## Energy Management

### The Energy Audit
Rate yourself 1–10 daily on:
- **Physical:** Sleep, nutrition, exercise, vitality
- **Mental:** Focus, clarity, learning, challenge
- **Emotional:** Joy, connection, gratitude, love
- **Spiritual:** Purpose, meaning, contribution, alignment

Your lowest score is dragging everything else down. Fix it first.

### The Transition Technique
Between meetings, tasks, or contexts:
1. **Release** — Let go of the last activity mentally (close your eyes, take 3 breaths)
2. **Set** — "What energy do I need to bring to this next activity?"
3. **Go** — Enter with intention, not carryover stress

**Why it matters:** Most entrepreneurs carry the stress of one task into the next all day. By the afternoon, they're operating at 30% capacity.

## Necessity (Why This Must Happen)

### Creating Internal Necessity
- Connect daily tasks to your deepest purpose
- "This sales call isn't just a sales call — it's my chance to change someone's business"
- Ask daily: "If I only had 6 months to build this, what would I do differently?"

### Creating External Necessity
- Public commitments (tell people your goals — now you have to deliver)
- Deadlines (real ones, not fake ones)
- Accountability partners or groups
- Clients depending on you

### The Performance Necessity Statement
"The people who need my help are counting on me to show up at my best today. I refuse to give them anything less than my full capability."

## Prolific Quality Output (PQO)

### Identify Your PQO
Every role has ONE output that matters more than all others.

| Role | PQO |
|---|---|
| Coach | Transformative sessions that create breakthroughs |
| Course Creator | Curriculum that produces measurable results |
| Agency Owner | Client campaigns that drive ROI |
| Consultant | Strategic recommendations that get implemented |
| Content Creator | Content that creates real engagement and leads |

### The PQO Time Block
- Block 2–4 hours daily for your PQO (protect this ruthlessly)
- Put it in your calendar like a meeting you can't cancel
- No email, no Slack, no calls during PQO time
- This is where your business actually grows

## Influence for Entrepreneurs

### The Influence Equation
**Influence = (Value Given × Consistency) + Connection**

- **Teach generously** — Share your best insights freely
- **Challenge respectfully** — Push people to raise their standards
- **Show up consistently** — Reliability builds trust faster than brilliance

### The "Ask" Hierarchy
1. Ask for attention (content)
2. Ask for engagement (comments, replies)
3. Ask for trust (email opt-in)
4. Ask for money (offer)
5. Ask for referrals (after delivering results)

Never skip levels. Earn each one.

## Courage for Entrepreneurs

### The Courage Gaps
Where entrepreneurs most commonly lack courage:
1. **Pricing** — Charging what the transformation is worth
2. **Selling** — Asking for the sale directly and confidently
3. **Firing** — Letting go of bad clients, wrong hires, broken partnerships
4. **Launching** — Shipping before it's "perfect"
5. **Leading** — Making unpopular decisions that serve the long-term vision
6. **Visibility** — Putting yourself out there publicly, consistently

### The Courage Formula
**Courage = Clarity of Purpose + Preparation + Practice**

You don't wait to feel courageous. You act courageously and the feeling follows.

## Key Insight
"High performance is not about working harder — it's about working with more clarity, more energy, more intention, and more courage. The entrepreneurs who win aren't the smartest or the luckiest. They're the ones who show up fully, every day, and do the work that matters."
