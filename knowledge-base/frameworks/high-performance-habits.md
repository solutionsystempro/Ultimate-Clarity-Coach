# High Performance Habits — Brendon Burchard

## Core Principle
High performance is not about talent, luck, or personality. It's about habits — specifically six habits that, when practiced deliberately, produce sustained success, health, and fulfillment. High performers don't just achieve more — they do it with more joy, confidence, and positive relationships.

## The 6 High Performance Habits

### Personal Habits (Internal)

#### 1. Seek Clarity
High performers are clear about who they want to be, how they want to interact, what they want, and what will bring meaning.

**Daily practice:**
- "Who do I need to be today?" (identity)
- "What matters most right now?" (focus)
- "What will make me feel alive?" (energy)

**For entrepreneurs:**
- Clarity on your offer, your ideal client, and your unique value
- Clarity on what you're building and why
- Clarity on what to say no to

#### 2. Generate Energy
High performers create and sustain extraordinary levels of mental, physical, and emotional energy.

**Daily practice:**
- **Release tension** — Set transitions between activities to reset
- **Set intention** — Before every meeting, call, or task: "What energy do I want to bring?"
- **Physical vitality** — Sleep, nutrition, exercise are non-negotiable performance tools

**For entrepreneurs:**
- Energy is your #1 business asset
- Burnout is not a badge of honor — it's a strategy failure
- Schedule recovery like you schedule meetings

#### 3. Raise Necessity
High performers feel a deep internal and external pressure to perform well — not from stress, but from identity, obligation, and standards.

**Daily practice:**
- Connect daily tasks to your larger purpose
- Make public commitments (accountability)
- Remember who is counting on you (family, clients, team)

**For entrepreneurs:**
- "Who needs me to show up at my best today?"
- Set deadlines that create healthy urgency
- Associate your performance with your identity: "This is who I am"

### Social Habits (External)

#### 4. Increase Productivity
High performers focus on "prolific quality output" (PQO) — the outputs that matter most in their field.

**Daily practice:**
- Identify the ONE output that drives everything else
- Time-block your most important work (protect it ruthlessly)
- Eliminate or delegate everything that isn't PQO

**For entrepreneurs:**
- What is your PQO? (offers created? sales calls? content? product development?)
- Put your best hours toward your highest-leverage activity
- "Busy" is not "productive" — measure outputs, not hours

#### 5. Develop Influence
High performers shape how others think, feel, and act — not through manipulation, but through teaching, challenging, and role-modeling.

**Daily practice:**
- **Teach** — Share what you know generously
- **Challenge** — Push people to rise, with care
- **Role model** — Be the standard you want to set

**For entrepreneurs:**
- Your ability to influence determines your ability to sell, lead, and grow
- Influence is earned through consistent value, not tactics
- Ask: "How can I help this person grow?" before every interaction

#### 6. Demonstrate Courage
High performers share their ideas, take bold action, and speak up for others — even when it's uncomfortable.

**Daily practice:**
- Share your real perspective, even when it's unpopular
- Take action before you feel ready
- Stand up for what you believe in, publicly

**For entrepreneurs:**
- Courage to charge what you're worth
- Courage to fire bad clients
- Courage to launch before it's "perfect"
- Courage to ask for the sale

## The Performance Mindset

- **Long-term success requires well-being.** High performance without health is a house of cards.
- **Confidence comes from competence.** Do the reps. Skills build certainty.
- **Progress = happiness.** If you're growing, you're thriving. If you're stagnating, you're suffering.
- **Environment accelerates everything.** Surround yourself with other high performers.

## Key Insight
"High performance is not about doing more. It's about doing what matters — with full engagement, clarity, and intention. The six habits are simple. The discipline to practice them daily is what separates the extraordinary from the average."
