# Massive Action Sales Framework — Grant Cardone

## Core Principle
Sales is a numbers game amplified by skill. Most salespeople and entrepreneurs fail not because their pitch is wrong, but because their VOLUME of activity is embarrassingly low. 10X your activity, and results follow.

## The Sales Activity Matrix

### The Math of Sales

If your close rate is 10%:
- 10 conversations = 1 sale
- 100 conversations = 10 sales
- 1,000 conversations = 100 sales

**The fastest way to 10X revenue:** 10X the number of conversations. Then improve the close rate.

### Daily Activity Targets (10X Standard)

| Activity | Average | 10X |
|---|---|---|
| Outreach attempts | 10/day | 100/day |
| Conversations | 3/day | 30/day |
| Proposals/offers | 1/day | 10/day |
| Follow-ups | 5/day | 50/day |
| Content pieces | 1/week | 1/day |

## The Follow-Up Formula

Most sales are lost because of lack of follow-up, not lack of interest.

**The stats:**
- 48% of salespeople never follow up
- 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups
- The average salesperson gives up after 2 attempts

**The Cardone Follow-Up Cadence:**
1. Immediately after first contact
2. 24 hours later
3. 48 hours later
4. 1 week later
5. 2 weeks later
6. Monthly until they buy, die, or tell you to stop

**Follow-up is not nagging — it's professional persistence.** The person who follows up the most wins.

## Phone & Conversation Framework

### The Power Close

1. **Greet with energy** — Your enthusiasm is contagious
2. **Agree with everything** — "You're right..." (then redirect)
3. **Find the pain** — "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"
4. **Amplify urgency** — "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 90 days?"
5. **Present the solution** — Direct, confident, no hedging
6. **Assume the close** — "Let's get you started. What email should I send the details to?"
7. **Handle objections** — "I understand. And that's exactly why you need this NOW."

### Rules of the Phone
- Stand up when you're on sales calls (energy transfers through your voice)
- Smile — it changes your tonality
- Never ask "Is this a good time?" — you're giving them permission to escape
- Mirror their pace, then lead them to higher energy
- First one to speak after the price loses

## Omnipresence Strategy

Be everywhere. The person people see the most becomes the person they trust the most.

**The formula:**
- Social media: Post 5–10x per day across all platforms
- Email: Daily (minimum)
- Video: Daily (short-form content)
- Podcast/interviews: Weekly
- Live events: Monthly
- Paid ads: Always running

**"Obscurity is the biggest threat to your business. Not competition. Not the economy. Obscurity."**

## Mindset of the 10X Salesperson

1. **Rejection is data, not failure.** Every "no" gets you closer to "yes."
2. **Money follows attention.** Whoever has the most attention wins.
3. **Sales is a service.** If you believe in your product, it's your moral obligation to sell it aggressively.
4. **Never lower the target.** If you're not hitting your number, increase the activity — never decrease the goal.
5. **Speed of implementation.** The gap between idea and action should be as small as possible.

## The Be-Do-Have Framework

- **Average person:** HAVE → DO → BE ("When I have money, I'll do the things I want, and I'll be happy")
- **10X person:** BE → DO → HAVE ("I'll BE the person who does massive action, I'll DO the work, and I'll HAVE the results")

Start being the person NOW. Don't wait for conditions to change.

## Key Insight
"You're not being outworked by your competition. You're being out-volumed. The person making 100 calls a day will always beat the person making 10 calls a day — even if the 10-call person has a 'better pitch.' Volume creates luck. Volume creates skill. Volume creates wealth."
