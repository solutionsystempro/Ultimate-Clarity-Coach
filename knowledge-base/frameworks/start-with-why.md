# Start With Why — Simon Sinek

## Core Principle
People don't buy what you do — they buy why you do it. The most inspiring leaders and organizations communicate from the inside out: Why → How → What. Most companies go outside in (What → How → Why) and wonder why nobody cares.

## The Golden Circle

### Why (Center)
Your purpose, cause, or belief. The reason your organization exists beyond making money.

- "Why do you get out of bed in the morning?"
- "Why should anyone care?"
- This is not "to make a profit" — that's a result, not a purpose

### How (Middle)
Your process, values, or unique approach. How you bring your Why to life.

- Your differentiating methodology
- The principles that guide your decisions
- Often expressed as a value proposition

### What (Outer)
Your products, services, and offerings. Every company knows what they do.

- This is the easiest to articulate
- But the least inspiring reason to buy

## The Biology of Why

- **Why** speaks to the limbic brain — the part that controls feelings, trust, loyalty, and decision-making. It has no capacity for language.
- **What** speaks to the neocortex — the rational, analytical brain.
- People make decisions emotionally (limbic) and justify rationally (neocortex).
- This is why features and specs rarely inspire — they speak to the wrong part of the brain.

## Application

### For Your Business

**Outside-In (Weak):**
"We make great project management software. It has Gantt charts, time tracking, and team collaboration. Want to buy it?"

**Inside-Out (Strong):**
"We believe small teams shouldn't need big bureaucracies to do great work. Everything we build removes friction and lets your team focus on what matters. One of the ways we do that is with software that's so simple, your team actually uses it."

### Finding Your Why

1. **Look backward, not forward.** Your Why comes from your story — what you've experienced, what you believe because of what you've lived.
2. **The contribution + impact formula:**
   - "To [contribution] so that [impact]"
   - Example: "To challenge people to think differently so that they can accomplish things they didn't think possible"
3. **It must be authentic.** You can't manufacture a Why — you can only discover it.

### For Leadership

- **Hire for Why, train for What.** Skills can be taught. Belief alignment can't.
- **The Law of Diffusion of Innovation:** You need to reach the innovators and early adopters (people who believe what you believe) before you can reach the majority.
- **The Celery Test:** If your Why is health, you buy celery, not M&Ms. Your Why filters every decision — products, hires, partnerships, marketing.

## The Infinite Game

- **Finite games** have known players, fixed rules, and agreed-upon objectives. The goal is to win.
- **Infinite games** have known and unknown players, changeable rules, and the objective is to keep playing.
- **Business is an infinite game.** Companies that play to "win" (beat the competition) eventually lose. Companies that play to advance a cause (their Why) outlast everyone.

### 5 Practices of Infinite-Minded Leaders
1. **Advance a Just Cause** — a vision of the future so compelling people sacrifice to advance it
2. **Build Trusting Teams** — psychological safety over performance pressure
3. **Study Worthy Rivals** — competitors reveal your weaknesses; learn, don't envy
4. **Prepare for Existential Flexibility** — willingness to pivot dramatically to advance the cause
5. **Demonstrate the Courage to Lead** — make the hard, unpopular decisions that serve the long game

## Key Insight
"People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it. And what you do simply proves what you believe. If you don't know why you do what you do, how will anyone else?"
