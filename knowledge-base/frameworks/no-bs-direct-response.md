# No B.S. Direct Response Marketing — Dan Kennedy

## Core Principle
Every dollar spent on marketing should be trackable to a specific result. No brand awareness for its own sake. No vanity metrics. Right message, right market, right media — and measure everything.

## The 3 M's of Marketing

### 1. Market (WHO)
Your ideal client — the person most likely to buy, most profitable to serve, and most enjoyable to work with.

**Define them precisely:**
- Demographics: age, income, location, industry
- Psychographics: fears, desires, frustrations, aspirations
- Buying behavior: what have they already bought? What are they buying now?
- Trigger events: what happens that makes them ready to buy?

**The "Herd" Principle:** People buy what people like them buy. Identify the herd. Speak their language. Show up where they gather.

### 2. Message (WHAT)
What you say to your market. Must be specific, benefit-driven, and compelling.

**The structure of a No B.S. message:**
1. **Headline** — Stop the right person with a specific promise or curiosity hook
2. **Problem agitation** — Make the pain vivid
3. **Solution presentation** — Your offer as the bridge
4. **Proof** — Testimonials, case studies, data
5. **Offer** — Exactly what they get, the price, the guarantee
6. **Call to action** — Clear, single, urgent next step
7. **Scarcity/urgency** — Real deadline or limited availability

### 3. Media (WHERE)
The channel through which your message reaches your market.

**Principles:**
- Go where your ideal client already is
- Don't follow trends — follow your market
- Test small, scale what works
- Multi-step sequences outperform one-shot attempts
- Direct mail is not dead — it's underused and therefore less competitive

## Key Copywriting Rules

1. **Write to one person.** Not "Dear valued customers." Speak to Steve, who's stressed about payroll on Friday.
2. **Lead with the benefit.** Not "Our proprietary system..." but "Double your appointments in 30 days."
3. **Long copy sells more than short copy.** If the reader is qualified, they want all the information.
4. **Every sentence must earn the next sentence.** Cut ruthlessly.
5. **Always include a call to action.** Every piece of marketing must ask for something specific.
6. **Deadline and scarcity are not optional.** Without urgency, "later" becomes "never."

## The Marketing Triangle Test

For any campaign, ask:
- **Market:** Am I reaching the right person?
- **Message:** Am I saying something that matters to them?
- **Media:** Am I using the right channel to find them?

If results are poor, one of these three is wrong. Test each independently.

## Key Insight
"The objective of marketing is not creativity, not awards, not likes, not brand awareness. The objective is to make the phone ring, the website convert, and the cash register sing. Everything else is expensive entertainment."
