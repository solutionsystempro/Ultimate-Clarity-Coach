# The 5-Step Sales Process — Eli Wilde

## Core Principle
"The person who is most certain will always influence the person who is least certain." Sales is not about pressure — it's about transferring certainty through a structured conversation.

## The 5 Steps

### Step 1: Build Rapport & Set the Frame
Establish trust and control the conversation frame from the start.

- **Mirror and match** — pace their energy, then lead
- **Set expectations** — "Here's how this conversation will go..."
- **Pattern interrupt** — break them out of "salesy" defense mode
- **Key question:** "What made you decide to take this call today?"

### Step 2: Identify the Problem (Deep Pain Discovery)
Go three levels deep. Surface pain is never the real pain.

- **Level 1:** What's the problem? (surface)
- **Level 2:** How does that make you feel? (emotional)
- **Level 3:** What does that mean for your future if nothing changes? (identity/existential)

**Key technique:** Silence after they answer. Let them sit in the pain. Don't rescue them.

### Step 3: Agitate & Amplify
Make the cost of inaction vivid and emotional.

- "What has this already cost you — in time, money, and stress?"
- "If you're in the same place 12 months from now, what does that look like?"
- "What's the real price of not solving this?"

**Key technique:** Calibrated empathy — show you understand, then intensify the stakes.

### Step 4: Present the Solution (Create Certainty)
Position your offer as the bridge from their pain to their dream outcome.

- **Paint the future:** "Imagine 90 days from now..."
- **Explain the mechanism:** WHY it works, not just WHAT it is
- **Social proof:** "Here's what happened for [similar client]..."
- **Unique value:** "The reason this is different is..."

**Key technique:** Certainty in your voice. No hedging. No "maybe." Speak as if the result is inevitable.

### Step 5: Close (Handle Objections by Dancing)
Objections are not walls — they are invitations to dance.

Common objections and reframes:
- **"I need to think about it"** → "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? Let's think through it together."
- **"It's too expensive"** → "Compared to what? What's the cost of staying where you are?"
- **"I need to talk to my partner"** → "Totally understand. If they said yes, would YOU be ready to move forward?"
- **"Now's not the right time"** → "When would be the right time? What changes between now and then?"

**Key technique:** Never argue. Agree, empathize, redirect. The dance, not the fight.

## The Certainty Scale

Rate your prospect's certainty 1–10 on three things:
1. **Certainty in the product/offer** — "Will this actually work?"
2. **Certainty in you/the company** — "Can I trust these people?"
3. **Certainty in themselves** — "Can I actually do this?"

If any of these is below a 7, that's where you focus. The close happens when all three hit 8+.

## Key Insight
"You don't have a closing problem. You have a certainty problem. Build certainty at every step, and the close takes care of itself."
