# The Grand Slam Offer — Alex Hormozi

## What Is a Grand Slam Offer?
An offer so good that people feel stupid saying no. It's not about discounting — it's about stacking so much value that the price becomes irrelevant.

## The Grand Slam Offer Formula

### Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome
What is the single most desirable result your customer wants?
- Be specific: not "grow your business" but "add $50K/month in recurring revenue in 90 days"
- The more vivid and measurable, the more valuable

### Step 2: List All the Problems
Write down every single obstacle, concern, and problem between where they are now and the dream outcome.
- What could go wrong?
- What have they tried before that failed?
- What are they afraid of?
- What don't they know how to do?
- What will be inconvenient or hard?

### Step 3: Turn Problems into Solutions
For each problem, create a specific solution:
- A course/training that teaches them
- A tool/template that does it for them
- A service that handles it entirely
- A community that supports them through it

### Step 4: Trim & Stack the Solutions
Pick the solutions that deliver the most value with the least cost to deliver. Stack them:

**Core Offer** — The main thing they're buying
**Bonus 1** — Solves a specific sub-problem (e.g., "Quick-Start Templates")
**Bonus 2** — Accelerates results (e.g., "1-on-1 Onboarding Call")
**Bonus 3** — Removes a key risk (e.g., "Done-For-You Setup")

### Step 5: Apply the Value Equation
Score each element: Does it increase Dream Outcome? Increase Perceived Likelihood? Decrease Time Delay? Decrease Effort?

### Step 6: Add a Guarantee
Types of guarantees:
- **Unconditional** — "Full refund, no questions asked, 30 days"
- **Conditional** — "Do the work, show up to calls, and if you don't get X result, full refund"
- **Anti-guarantee** — "All sales final — this is for serious people only" (scarcity play)
- **Outcome-based** — "We work for free until you hit $X"

### Step 7: Name It
A great offer name includes:
- A unique mechanism or branded method
- A specific result or timeframe
- An identity element

**Examples:**
- "The 90-Day Revenue Accelerator"
- "The Offer Architect Program"
- "The $100K Launch Blueprint"

## Pricing Psychology
- Price communicates value — higher prices attract better clients
- The goal is NOT to be cheap — it's to make the value so obvious that the price is a bargain
- If no one is complaining about price, you're probably too cheap
- 3–10x what you think you should charge is usually closer to right

## Key Insight
"The goal is not to make a good offer. The goal is to make an offer so good that it would be irrational to say no. When you do that, sales becomes order-taking."
