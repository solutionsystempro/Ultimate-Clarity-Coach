# Magnetic Marketing — Dan Kennedy

## Core Principle
Stop chasing customers. Attract them. The most profitable businesses don't prospect — they create marketing so targeted and compelling that ideal clients come to them, pre-sold and ready to buy.

## The Magnetic Marketing Triangle

### 1. The Right Message
Not clever. Not creative. SPECIFIC and COMPELLING.

**Message Formula:**
- **Headline:** Interrupt the right person with their #1 desire or pain
- **Body:** Agitate the problem → Present your unique solution → Prove it works
- **CTA:** One clear, specific, urgent next step

**Writing rules:**
- Write to ONE person, not an audience
- Use their words, not your jargon
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Long copy outperforms short copy (for qualified prospects)
- Every sentence must earn the next

### 2. The Right Market
The most important decision in your business: WHO you serve.

**The Ideal Client Avatar:**
- **Demographic:** Age, income, location, industry, role
- **Psychographic:** Fears, desires, frustrations, beliefs, values
- **Behavioral:** What do they already buy? Where do they spend time? What do they read?
- **Situational:** What trigger event makes them ready to buy NOW?

**The "Herd" Concept:**
People buy what people like them buy. Identify the herd → Speak their language → Show up where they gather → Become the trusted advisor of that herd.

**The Ideal Client Filter:**
- Can they afford your solution?
- Do they have the authority to say yes?
- Do they have the problem you solve?
- Is the problem urgent?
- Are they pleasant to work with?

If the answer to any of these is "no" — disqualify and move on.

### 3. The Right Media
The channel that reaches your market, delivers your message, and produces measurable results.

**Kennedy's Media Hierarchy:**
1. **Direct mail** — Least competition, highest response rates, most personal
2. **Email** — Fast, free, scalable — but crowded
3. **Phone/Outbound** — Highest conversion, hardest to scale
4. **In-person events** — Highest trust, most persuasive
5. **Digital ads** — Scalable, targetable, but expensive and commoditized
6. **Social media** — Good for omnipresence, bad for direct response (usually)

**Key rule:** Test your message on the cheapest media first. Only scale what produces measurable ROI.

## The "No B.S." Client Attraction System

### Step 1: Lead Generation
Create a "bait piece" (lead magnet) that only attracts your ideal client.

**Examples:**
- "The 7 Profit Leaks Draining Your [Industry] Business"
- "How [Specific Type of Business] Can Add $100K Without More Customers"
- "The [Industry] Owner's Guide to Escaping the Revenue Plateau"

### Step 2: Indoctrination
Once they opt in, educate and position yourself as the authority.

**Sequence:**
- Email 1: Deliver the bait piece + your story
- Email 2: Case study / proof
- Email 3: Teach something valuable
- Email 4: Address the #1 objection
- Email 5: Make the offer with urgency

### Step 3: Conversion
Make a specific offer with a deadline.

**Must include:**
- Clear description of what they get
- Price + value comparison (10x value to price)
- Guarantee
- Deadline or scarcity (real — not fake)
- Clear CTA

### Step 4: Ascension
Once they buy, immediately offer the next level:
- "Now that you have X, the next step is Y..."
- Backend offers are where the real money is
- Your best client is someone who already bought

## The Marketing Budget Rule

"If you can't measure it, don't spend it."

Every marketing dollar must be tracked to:
- Cost per lead
- Cost per appointment
- Cost per sale
- Lifetime value of customer
- ROI per campaign

**No vanity metrics.** Likes, views, and followers don't pay bills. Sales do.

## Key Insight
"The purpose of marketing is not to be liked, to win awards, or to go viral. The purpose of marketing is to make the cash register ring. If your marketing isn't producing measurable results, it's not marketing — it's expensive entertainment."
